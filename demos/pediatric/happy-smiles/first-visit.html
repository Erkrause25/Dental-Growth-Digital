Your Child's First Visit

We believe first impressions matter. Learn what to expect and how to prepare for a positive, fun-filled dental experience.

When Should My Child First See a Dentist?

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends that children visit a dentist by their first birthday or within 6 months after their first tooth appearsâ€”whichever comes first.

Early visits allow us to monitor your child's oral development, identify potential issues before they become problems, and establish a "dental home" where your child feels comfortable.

Starting early helps prevent cavities and sets the stage for a lifetime of healthy smiles!

What to Expect

Your Visit Step by Step

We've designed every step of the first visit to be welcoming and stress-free.

1

Warm Welcome

When you arrive, our friendly team will greet you and your child by name. We'll give your little one a tour of our office, let them explore the play area, and introduce them to our kid-friendly environment before any treatment begins.

2

Getting to Know You

We'll sit down with you to discuss your child's health history, any concerns you have, and answer all your questions. This helps us understand your child's unique needs and build a trusting relationship.

3

Gentle Examination

Our dentist will gently examine your child's teeth, gums, and jaw. For young children, this often happens while they sit in a parent's lap. We use child-friendly language and take our time to ensure comfort.

4

Cleaning & Fluoride

If your child is ready, we'll do a gentle cleaning to remove any plaque buildup and apply a fluoride treatment to strengthen their teeth. We use fun-flavored fluoride that kids love!

5

Education & Planning

We'll discuss our findings, provide tips for home care appropriate to your child's age, and create a plan for future visits. You'll leave knowing exactly how to keep those little teeth healthy.

6

Prize Time!

Every visit ends on a high note. Your child gets to pick a prize from our treasure chestâ€”a fun reward for being such a great patient!

Before Your Visit

How to Prepare Your Child

A little preparation can make a big difference in how your child experiences their first visit.

Do These Things

  • Read books about visiting the dentist together
  • Play "dentist" at home with a toothbrush and mirror
  • Use positive words like "strong," "healthy," and "clean"
  • Schedule the appointment when your child is well-rested
  • Bring a comfort item like a favorite toy or blanket

Avoid These Things

  • Don't use the dentist as a threat or punishment
  • Avoid words like "hurt," "shot," "drill," or "pain"
  • Don't share your own dental anxieties with your child
  • Don't make promises about what will or won't happen
  • Avoid scheduling when your child is tired or hungry

Tips for a Successful Visit

Stay Positive

Your attitude matters! Children pick up on parental anxiety. Stay calm, cheerful, and confident about the visit.

Trust Our Team

Common Questions

First Visit FAQs

Here are answers to questions we hear most often from parents.

How long will the first visit take?

Plan for about 45 minutes to an hour. This gives us time to make your child comfortable, complete the exam, do a cleaning if appropriate, and answer all your questions without feeling rushed.

Can I stay with my child during the appointment?

Absolutely! Parents are always welcome in the treatment area. For very young children, we often do "lap-to-lap" exams where your child sits in your lap facing the dentist. Your presence can be very comforting.

What if my child cries or doesn't cooperate?

This is completely normal and expected, especially for young children! Our team is highly trained in behavior guidance techniques. We never force treatment and will work at your child's pace. Sometimes it takes a few visits to build comfort, and that's perfectly okay.

Will my child need X-rays?

It depends on your child's age and risk factors. We only recommend X-rays when necessary and use digital technology that minimizes radiation exposure. For very young children at the first visit, we often don't need X-rays at all.

What should I bring to the appointment?

Please bring your insurance card, any completed forms (available for download below), a list of medications your child takes, and your child's comfort item if they have one. If you have specific concerns or questions, write them down so you don't forget!
